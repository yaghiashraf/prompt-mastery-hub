Zero-Shot vs. Few-Shot: The ROI of Examples

Jan 12, 2026 7 min read Strategy

You can write 500 words of instructions (Zero-Shot) or you can provide 3 examples (Few-Shot). The examples will win 9 times out of 10.

Definitions

  • Zero-Shot: Asking the model to do something without examples. (e.g., "Classify this tweet.")
  • One-Shot: Providing one example.
  • Few-Shot: Providing 3-5 examples of input -> output pairs.

The Performance Gap

Research from the GPT-3 paper showed that on complex reasoning tasks, Few-Shot prompting can improve accuracy from <20% to over 80%.

Why? Because examples serve as implicit instructions. They demonstrate tone, format, length, and edge-case handling without you having to explicitly describe them.

When to use which?

Use Zero-Shot when:

  • The task is simple (Summarization, Translation).
  • You want maximum creativity/variance.
  • You are hitting context window limits.

Use Few-Shot when:

  • You need structured output (JSON, SQL).
  • The task involves nuance (Sarcasm, Legal classification).
  • You need to copy a specific style.
